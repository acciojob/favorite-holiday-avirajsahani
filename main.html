Jammu Kashmir

Jammu and Kashmir (J&K) is a region administered by India as a union territory. It comprises the southern portion of the larger Kashmir region, which has been a point of territorial conflict since 1947. The Line of Control separates it from Pakistani-administered areas, while to the north and east lie the Indian states of Himachal Pradesh, Punjab, and the union territory of Ladakh. Known for its breathtaking landscapes, spiritual sites, and cultural richness, Jammu and Kashmir is a truly enchanting destination for travelers.

  1. Srinagar – Famous for its scenic lakes, gardens, and traditional houseboats.
  2. Dal Lake – Known for shikara rides and floating markets, it’s a must-see attraction.
  3. Shankaracharya Temple – A historic site offering panoramic views of Srinagar.
  4. Pahalgam – A picturesque town surrounded by lush greenery and rivers.
  5. Aru Valley – A peaceful valley ideal for trekking, camping, and photography.
  • Rogan Josh – A rich, flavorful lamb curry cooked with aromatic spices.
  • Modur Pulao – A sweet Kashmiri rice dish with saffron and dry fruits.
  • Gushtaba – Minced meatballs in a creamy yogurt gravy, served during special occasions.
  • Jalebi – A classic Indian sweet made from deep-fried spirals soaked in sugar syrup.
  • Aamras – A dessert made from sweet mango pulp, typically served with puris.
